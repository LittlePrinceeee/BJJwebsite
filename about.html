Basic Things in BJJ

Some terms you should know in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu include:

  1. Guard: A position where the practitioner is on their back and uses their legs to control the opponent.
  2. Mount: A dominant position where the practitioner sits on top of the opponent's chest.
  3. Side Control: A position where the practitioner controls the opponent from the side, often used to set up submissions.
  4. Back Take: A technique used to transition from a dominant position to a back control position.
  5. Submission: A technique used to force an opponent to submit, such as a choke or joint lock.

Basic Submissions

There are several fundamental submissions for beginners:

  • Rear Naked Choke: A submission technique where the practitioner wraps their arm around the opponent's neck from behind and applies pressure to choke them.
  • Armbar: A submission technique that extends the opponent's arm, causing pain and potential to break opponent's arm.
  • Kimura Lock: A shoulder lock submission that can be applied from various positions.
  • Triangle Choke: A submission technique where the practitioner uses their legs to encircle the opponent's neck and one arm, cutting off blood flow and causing a choke.
